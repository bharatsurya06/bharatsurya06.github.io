Portable Thermoelectric Cooler (Prototype)

Overview

Thermocletric effect is a phenomenon where a voltage is generated across the a semiconductor junction when a temperature difference is applied. The corrollary of this effect, known as the peltier effect is used to cool electronic components by applying a voltage across the junction. These peltier devices are simple to operate, compact and contain no moving parts which make them ideal choices for portable cooling applications. In this project, a working prototype of a thermoclectic cooler were developed and demonstrated. This device operated on a 12 V power supply and was able to cool down a small volume of container to temperatures below ambinet temperature.
