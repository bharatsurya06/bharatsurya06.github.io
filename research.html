Cryogenic energy recovery and storage





Refrigeration and cooling demands acount for more than 10% of the greenhouse emissions globally. These demands include sectors such as cold chain, space cooling, industrial gas liquefaction and production, at different temperature levels. Usually, conventional refirgeration such as VCR, VAR whihc use CFC, HFCs posessing high GWP are used for meeting these demands. Inaddition, industrial gas liquefaction processes draw vast amounts of energy for compressing the gases to high pressures and cool them down to cryogenic temepratures. Researchers has been actively involved in reducing the power consumption by through design, development of less energy intensive and through process modifications and integrations. One of the areas where the sector seems vast potential is in the ragsification of liquid cryogens.
