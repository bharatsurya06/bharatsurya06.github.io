Regasification of cryogens

The regasification process of liquid cryogens such as LNG, liquid air, LOX, LN2, is associated with release of ultra-low temperature thermal energy ( ~ 110 K). We refer to this energy as cryogenic cold energy. On the other hand, ultra-low temperature refrigeration or cooling is a necessity in inudstrial processes such as air separation, carbon capture, gas liquefaction, recent additions being the vaccine cold chain, warehouse cooling. Suitable energy recovery and storage systems enable a flexible intergation of the source cryogenic cold energy with the demand of an application. These systems are callled cryogenic cold energy storage systems, and are specifically meant to store energy during the times of no demand.
