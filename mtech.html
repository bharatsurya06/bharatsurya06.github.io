Investigation of chilldown in a LIN transfer line

Chilldown of cryogenic equipment such as storage tanks and transfer lines is a critical process which happens during start-up of cryogenic systems. As the name suggets, chilldowwn represents the intial cool down period of the cryogenic equipment such from their inital temperature to the operating temperature of the flowing liquid cryogen. By the end of this period, the liquid cryogen flows through the system steadily and the system is ready for normal operation. The chilldown presents a critical stage in the operation of cryogenic systems, as it involves complex transient thermal phenomena, including rapid cooling, phase change and thermal stresses during the flow. It leads to various types of two phase flow phenomena as the liquid trasnitions from various boiling regrimes. Charecterizing the chilldown behaviour is important for optimizing the start-up procedures, ensuring safe operation, i.e, pressure fluctuations and minimal loss of cryogen.
